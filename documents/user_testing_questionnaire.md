## Section F — Scoring Sheet (Chinelo only — do NOT send)

### SUS score (per participant)
1. Odd items (1,3,5,7,9):  score = answer − 1
2. Even items (2,4,6,8,10): score = 5 − answer
3. Sum all 10 converted scores, multiply by 2.5 → 0–100.
4. Average across participants = final SUS.

Grade bands: ≥ 84.1 A+ · 80.8–84 A · 78.9–80.7 A− · 77.2–78.8 B+ ·
74.1–77.1 B · 72.6–74 B− · 71.1–72.5 C+ · 65–71 C · 62.7–64.9 C− ·
51.7–62.6 D · below 51.7 F. (68 = industry average.)

### Task success rate
Each participant attempted 3 tasks → total tasks = 3 × participants.
Success rate = (tasks completed WITHOUT help) ÷ total tasks.
Report "with help" completions separately — they reveal usability
issues even when the task eventually succeeded.

### Top 3 usability findings
Look for patterns across B (where they needed help), E2 (confusion),
and E4 (missing). A finding = "N of M users struggled with X →
proposed fix Y."

### Quotes
Pull verbatim from E1/E3. You need at least 1 per participant for the
deliverable; the E3 one-sentence descriptions are usually the most
presentation-worthy.

### Slide-ready summary template
- Participants: N TikTok creators, X–Y followers (real target users)
- SUS: __ / 100 (Grade _)
- Task success: __% without help (__% incl. helped)
- Top 3 findings: 1) … 2) … 3) …
- Quote: "…" — participant N
- D6 average ("would change what I post"): __ / 5 ← this is your
  product-value headline, separate from usability.
